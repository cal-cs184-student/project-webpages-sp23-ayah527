Visualizing Theoretical Physics: Human Traversable Wormholes

  • Summary: We render the theoretical physical construct, a wormhole, which connects two distance points in space. We render one large enough to be traversable by humans, though one of this size is likely impossible (or dependent on unknown physical phenomena, such as dark energy).

  • Problem Description
    • We are using current knowledge about theoretical physics to construct a visualization of it. In terms of creating this image, the most difficult parts are going to be changing our current ray tracing implementation to support and accurately render two different scenes or images together in accordance with the math from our resources. 
    • The idea was inspired by the film Interstellar, which includes a high quality rendering of a wormhole. Kip Thorne, a physicist, wrote multiple papers on the subject and assisted in the production of Interstellar’s renderings. We use his work as a reference.
    • Renderings of theoretical physical phenomena have the potential to increase our intuition and understanding of the subject, and attract more students to the subject.
